Projects

I am actively involved in two major European research projects that apply AI and privacy-preserving machine learning techniques in real-world settings:

  • DADAP Project: Developing an AI model for mental health disorders using federated learning. The project enables on-site psychiatric diagnostics while ensuring privacy through personalized differential privacy.
  • DAIS Project: Focused on applying privacy-preserving techniques — including differential privacy, homomorphic encryption, and functional encryption — in federated learning for industrial use-cases, with an emphasis on balancing privacy and performance.
